# Northwind Robotics — Company Overview

Northwind Robotics was founded in 2018 in Bristol, UK, by a team of former
industrial automation engineers. The company designs and manufactures modular
warehouse robots used for picking, packing, and inventory scanning in
mid-to-large distribution centers.

## Mission

Our mission is to make warehouse automation accessible to mid-sized logistics
companies that cannot afford the large, custom robotic fleets sold by bigger
competitors. We do this by offering modular hardware that can be leased,
financed, or purchased outright, paired with a subscription software platform
called NorthwindOS.

## Leadership

- CEO: Priya Anand — co-founder, previously led robotics R&D at a major
  automotive supplier.
- CTO: Marcus Webb — co-founder, background in computer vision and SLAM
  navigation systems.
- VP of Customer Success: Elena Ruiz — joined in 2021, oversees the support
  and onboarding teams described in the support policy.

## Offices

Northwind Robotics has its headquarters and main manufacturing facility in
Bristol, UK, with a secondary support and logistics office in Krakow, Poland,
which handles European customer support during UK night hours.

## Company size

As of the most recent fiscal year, Northwind Robotics employs approximately
340 people across engineering, manufacturing, sales, and support. Roughly 90
of those employees are part of the engineering organization referenced in the
engineering handbook.

## Funding

The company raised a Series B round of $42 million in 2023, led by a
European industrial-tech venture fund, to expand manufacturing capacity and
grow the NorthwindOS software team.
